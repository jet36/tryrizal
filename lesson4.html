Rizal’s Life: Family, Childhood, Early Education

Jose Protacio Mercado Rizal Alonzo y Realonda was born on June 19, 1861, between 11 pm and 12 am, in Calamba, Laguna. He had a diverse heritage that included Chinese, Malay, Indonesian, and Japanese ancestry. His father, Don Francisco Mercado, built a nipa hut, while his mother, Doña Teodora Alonzo, taught him the Angelus and Catholic teachings. Despite being sick and frail, Rizal came from a notable lineage, with his great-great-grandfather, Domingo Lam-co, being a Chinese immigrant and business tycoon.

Dr. Jose Rizal's Siblings

Saturnina Hidalgo


Saturnina Hidalgo


Saturnina Rizal Mercado de Hidalgo, or simply Saturnina Hidalgo, was the eldest sister of Philippine national hero José Rizal.

Paciano Rizal


Paciano Rizal


Paciano Rizal Mercado y Alonso Realonda was a Filipino general and revolutionary, and the older brother of José Rizal, the national hero of the Philippines.

Narcisa Rizal


Narcisa Rizal


Narcisa Rizal Lopez was born in 1852 and was the one who found the unmarked grave of her brother, Jose in the abandoned Old Paco Cemetery. Narcisa married Antonio Lopez who was a teacher and musician from Morong, Rizal.

Olympia Rizal


Olympia Rizal


Olympia Rizal Mercado (1855-1887), was Jose Rizal Mercado's sister. She was the fourth child in the Rizal Mercado family. She was the fourth child in the Rizal Mercado family and was a custodian of Rizal's last and greatest poem

Lucia Alonso Herbosa


Lucia Alonso Herbosa


Lucia Rizal (1857–1919) is the fifth child in the Rizal family. Charged of inciting the Calamba townsfolk not to pay land rent and causing unrest, the couple was once ordered to be deported along with some Rizal family members.

Maria Rizal


Maria Rizal


Maria Rizal is the sixth child in the family. It was to her whom Jose talked about wanting to marry Josephine Bracken when the majority of the Rizal family was apparently not amenable to the idea.

Concepcion Rizal


Concepcion Rizal


Concepcion Rizal, also called 'Concha' by her siblings, was the eight child of the Rizal family. She died at the age of three. Of his sisters, it is said that Pepe loved most the little Concha who was a year younger than him.

Josefa Rizal


Josefa Rizal


She was one of the original 29 women Josefa Rizal, one of the original 29 women admitted to the Katipunan along with Gregoria de Jesus, wife of Andres Bonifacio Trinidad Rizal, historically, she became the custodian of Rizal's last and greatest poem.

Trinidad Rizal


Trinidad Rizal


Trinidad Rizal y Alonso, commonly known as Trinidad Rizal, was a Filipina feminist leader and co-founder of the Philippines' first feminist organization, the Asociación Femenista Filipina. She was the tenth sibling of the national hero, physician and writer, Dr. José Rizal.

Soledad Rizal


Soledad Rizal


Also called ‘Choleng,’ Soledad Rizal (1870-1929) was the youngest child of the Rizal family. Being a teacher, she was arguably the best educated among Rizal’s sisters.

Biñan, Laguna

In 1869, under the guidance of his older brother Paciano, who acted as a second father to him, Jose Rizal began his formal education in Biñan with Maestro Justiniano Aquino Cruz as his first formal teacher. Rizal excelled in his studies, particularly in Spanish and Latin, surpassing all his subjects. His education focused on reading, writing, arithmetic, and religion, laying a strong foundation for his future intellectual pursuits.
