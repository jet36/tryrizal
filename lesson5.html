Rizal continued his education abroad, studying medicine in Spain and ophthalmology in Paris. He traveled extensively throughout Europe, visiting cities like Barcelona, Madrid, Paris, Berlin, Dresden, Prague, Vienna, and Geneva. In Asia, he briefly stopped in Singapore on his way to Spain and later visited Hong Kong and Japan. Throughout his travels, Rizal keenly observed the cultures, governments, and societies of the places he visited in order to prepare for his goal of liberating the Philippines from Spanish rule.

JOSÉ RIZAL, A FILIPINO NATIONALIST AND REVOLUTIONARY, SPENT A SIGNIFICANT PORTION OF HIS LIFE ABROAD.

Early Education and Initial Travels:

Spain (1882-1885)

Rizal traveled to Madrid to study medicine at the Universidad Central de Madrid. Here, he also pursued studies in philosophy and letters. This period was crucial for his exposure to liberal ideas and European intellectual movements.

France and Germany (1885-1887)

Rizal moved to Paris to further his medical studies under the renowned ophthalmologist Dr. Louis de Wecker. He then went to Heidelberg, Germany, to complete his ophthalmology training.

Political Activities and Publications:

Berlin (1887)

Rizal published *Noli Me Tangere*, a novel that criticized Spanish colonial rule and the Catholic Church in the Philippines. The book played a crucial role in the Filipino reform movement.

Europe (1887-1892)

Rizal traveled extensively across Europe, visiting countries like Italy, Switzerland, and Austria. He met with fellow reformists and continued his writings. In 1891, he published his second novel, *El Filibusterismo*, in Ghent, Belgium, further advocating for Filipino rights and reforms.

Return to the Philippines, Imprisonment, and Execution

Philippines (1892)

Rizal returned to the Philippines, founded the La Liga Filipina, a reformist society aiming for peaceful change. However, his activities led to his arrest and exile to Dapitan in Mindanao

Dapitan (1892-1896)

During his exile in Dapitan, Rizal continued his medical practice, conducted scientific studies, and engaged in community projects. He also met Josephine Bracken, who would become his partner.

Manila (1896)

Rizal was tried for rebellion, sedition, and conspiracy. He was executed by firing squad on December 30, 1896, at Bagumbayan (now Luneta Park), cementing his status as a national hero of the Philippines.
